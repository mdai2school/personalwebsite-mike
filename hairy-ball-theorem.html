>> THE HAIRY BALL THEOREM _

>> TL;DR

  • You cannot comb a sphere smoothly—every continuous tangent vector field on S² hits a zero.
  • The obstruction is topological: non-zero tangent fields only exist on odd-dimensional spheres.
  • Real-world echo: at any moment there is at least one calm point in global wind or ocean flow data.

>> FORMULA SNAPSHOT

A concrete tangent field that makes the theorem visible is:

F(x, y, z) = (-y, x, 0), for (x, y, z) ∈ S²

The dot product F · (x, y, z) = 0 keeps the arrows tangent. At the poles, where x = y = 0, the vector collapses to 0—the unavoidable bald spot.
